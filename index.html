08 — Synthesis

Where the real gap is — and isn't

Formester is a form builder with one gated scheduling add-on; Calendly is a dedicated scheduling product with a forms-adjacent layer bolted on. The gap is structural, concentrated in three places, with a couple of areas where Formester is actually ahead.

Scheduling depth

  • No native event-type object (1:1 is a manual one-form-per-meeting-type workaround); group bookings are natively supported (multi-seat slots), but collective/round-robin have no equivalent
  • No native calendar sync or double-booking prevention
  • No managed/locked meeting templates; meeting polls are partial (counts exist, no live inline tally)

Team & admin tooling

  • No analytics dashboard in the plan/subscription schema
  • No group structure or delegated admins
  • SSO/SCIM absent outright, not merely ungated

Reach & channel

  • No mobile app or browser extensions
  • No Contacts/CRM layer
  • Routing stops at HubSpot — no Salesforce or Marketo

Where Formester already wins

  • Monday.com, ADF, and Databricks integrations — absent from Calendly entirely
  • Higher disclosed webhook ceilings per plan
  • Embed widgets on par with Calendly's
  • Native group booking (N-seat slots, shared calendar event) — on par with Calendly's group event type
09 — Recommendations

Plan & pricing best practices worth adopting

Fix this regardless of Calendly A prod rake task (plans:raise_limits) set form and submission limits to the same near-unlimited number on every paid tier, while the pricing page copy still advertises differentiated caps ("1k/mo" vs "15k/mo"). That mismatch is a trust risk independent of any competitor comparison.
  • Consolidate tiers. Three tiers is the sweet spot for B2B SaaS decision-making; Formester currently carries 13 plan rows in production, most of them legacy or one-off (LTD, non-profit, developer).
  • Lean into hybrid pricing. ~43% of SaaS companies already blend tiered and usage-based pricing, trending toward 61% by end of 2026, and it correlates with the highest median growth. Formester's per-org custom_form_limit/custom_submission_limit overrides are a ready foundation for usage-based upsells.
  • Gate by persona, not just price. Calendly separates "power-user" features (Standard+) from "team-admin" features (Teams+). Formester's flat per-feature boolean columns could be organized the same way when designing a future Team tier.
  • Version the feature flags. Plan features are unversioned DB columns edited by ad hoc rake tasks with no audit trail — unlike Subscription, which already uses paper_trail. Treating entitlements as versioned config would let support and pricing experiments answer "what did Business include on date X."
